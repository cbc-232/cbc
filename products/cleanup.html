The Problem

Waste mishandled. Streets unsafe. Communities left without a solution.

In Sierra Leone, households face a tough choice: rely on children to carry waste or turn to untrusted collectors, often drug addicts, who dump waste in the wrong places. Streets, rivers, and open areas become polluted, and public health is at risk.

Tricycle owners, while trusted, are not always accessible to every home, and services can be costly. Some households can afford them, some cannot — leaving gaps in the system that create chaos and environmental hazards.

This fragmented setup leaves families stressed, neighborhoods unsanitary, and city authorities struggling to manage waste. The current process is unorganized, unsafe, and unsustainable.

Communities deserve a system that is trustworthy, accessible, and efficient — a solution that ensures waste goes where it should, without relying on unsafe shortcuts.

FAQs

Get direct answers about CleanUp — how it works, who it supports, and how it’s transforming waste management in Sierra Leone.

What exactly is CleanUp?

CleanUp is a digital platform that connects households to trusted, verified tricycle operators, ensures waste reaches registered dump sites, and links with local authorities. It’s designed to make waste collection reliable, safe, and affordable, replacing unsafe or inconsistent informal practices.

Who is CleanUp for?

CleanUp serves households who want dependable waste collection, tricycle operators seeking steady and structured work, and city authorities monitoring sanitation. It works for families with limited budgets, homes seeking safer disposal, and operators aiming to reach more areas efficiently.

Can I use CleanUp without a smartphone?

Absolutely. CleanUp supports USSD and basic phones, ensuring households can book waste collection and stay updated even if they don’t have a smartphone. Accessibility is a key part of our design, so no one is left out of the system.

Is CleanUp already available?

CleanUp is currently in development with pilot programs running in selected wards. A full public launch is coming soon, and you can join the waitlist to be among the first households and operators using the platform.

How is CleanUp different from informal waste collection?

Unlike informal or unsafe collectors, CleanUp connects households to verified tricycle operators who transport waste to registered dump sites. The system ensures transparency, affordability, and efficiency, replacing chaos with a structured, reliable process that benefits the entire community.

How can I get involved?

You can join CleanUp’s pilot programs, sign up for early updates, or partner with us as a household, operator, or authority. Every participant helps create a cleaner, safer, and more organized urban environment.
